Netherlands Residence Permit for Graduate Students Outside Netherlands

Hello everyone! Did you know that the Netherlands 🇳🇱 gives residence permit to graduate students outside the Netherlands as highly educated migrants seeking employment? I will explain the requirements for this residence permit and provide some scenarios to make you understand better.

The Netherlands government has been trying to attract highly skilled migrants with immigrant-friendly laws and tax breaks. Therefore, it allows people from other countries to move to the Netherlands with various residence permits or visas. One of them is a ‘Residence permit for orientation year as a highly educated migrant seeking employment.’

What is a residence permit for orientation year as a highly educated immigrant?

Residence permit for orientation year as highly educated immigrants is a residence permit type came into effect on 1 March 2016, and that highly educated migrants take enough time to seek a job or set up a start-up in the Netherlands. This allows immigrants to settle in the Netherlands even if they have not found a job initially. They can work in the Netherlands in temporary jobs or as freelancers, set up start-ups and do not need companies to sponsor them for work permit as long as the residence permit lasts. Moreover, it is called orientation year because migrants are expected to apply within a certain time after graduation.

Highly educated immigrants consist of three groups:

  • foreign students studying in the Netherlands;
  • foreign students who graduated from a top university outside the Netherlands;
  • foreign scientific researchers who have carried out research in the Netherlands.

This article will consider foreign students who graduated from a top university outside the Netherlands. If you are in other groups, you can view the details on the official website page of this residence permit.

Requirements for foreign students who graduated from a top university outside the Netherlands are:

  • Meeting general requirements for everyone
  • A master’s degree program, a doctoral program, or a post-master program at a designated foreign educational institution
  • Being within three years after graduation
  • Proof of English proficiency

What is English Proficiency?

There are two versions of the IELTS: Academic and General Training. Both are valid for the application.

Is my university eligible to apply?

Your university must be a designated foreign educational institution. The Netherlands Immigration and Naturalization Service explains these institutions as universities that rank in the top 200 in a list published by some publishers.

These publishers are:

  • Times Higher Education
  • Quacquarelli Symonds (QS)
  • ShanghaiRanking Consultancy

These three institutions publish rankings of universities every year according to specific evaluation criteria. There are two types of ranking:

  • General Ranking: General ranks of universities
  • By Subject Ranking: The ranks of universities in particular fields, such as computer science or business and economics.

In order to be considered as a designated foreign educational institution, your university must be:

  • Ranked in the top 200 in a particular academic subject(by subject) or in general by at least 2 of the publishers above.
  • Meet the above condition in the rankings published in the year you graduated.

Remember, rankings published by the same publisher count as one.

Final Thoughts

The Residence Permit for Highly Educated Migrants Seeking Employment in the Netherlands is an excellent opportunity for graduates from top international universities. If you’ve completed a master’s, doctoral, or post-master’s program and are proficient in English, this orientation year can be your ticket to new opportunities.

This summary touches on the basics, so it’s crucial to check the official website of the Netherlands Immigration and Naturalization Service and FAQ section for the most up-to-date and detailed information. You’ll find everything you need to know about the application process there.
